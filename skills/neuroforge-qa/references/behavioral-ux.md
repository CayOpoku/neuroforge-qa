# Behavioral Design & Persuasion Frameworks

Beyond usability — how to influence behavior and increase conversion.

---

## 🚀 Fogg Behavior Model
**Formula:** `B = MAP` (Behavior happens when **Motivation**, **Ability**, and a **Prompt** converge).

- **Motivation:** Does the user WANT to do it? (Pleasure/Pain, Hope/Fear, Acceptance/Rejection).
- **Ability:** Is it EASY to do? (Time, Money, Physical Effort, Brain Cycles, Social Deviance, Non-Routine).
- **Prompt:** Is there a clear trigger to act NOW?

---

## 🪝 Nir Eyal's Hook Model
For habit-forming products:
1. **Trigger:** Internal (itch) or External (notification).
2. **Action:** The simplest behavior in anticipation of a reward.
3. **Variable Reward:** Fulfils the itch but leaves the user wanting more.
4. **Investment:** Small bit of work (data, time, effort) that improves the product for next time.

---

## 🧠 Nudge Theory & Behavioral Science
- **Defaulting:** Users rarely change default settings. Use "Smart Defaults" to guide them.
- **Loss Aversion:** Users fear losing something more than they value gaining something.
- **Social Proof:** "1,000 others bought this" or "Verified Expert".
- **Scarcity/Urgency:** "Only 2 left at this price." (Use ethically).
- **Framing Effect:** How information is presented (90% Fat-Free vs. 10% Fat).

---

## 🎯 Conversion Optimization (CRO) Checklist
- **Single Objective:** Every page should have ONE primary goal.
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Does the primary CTA stand out? (Von Restorff Effect).
- **Cognitive Load:** Are we asking for too much info too early? (Hick's Law).
- **Trust Signals:** SSL badges, testimonials, professional aesthetic.
- **Clear Value Prop:** Is it obvious what the benefit is in <5 seconds?
